# Interview Guides

## Study setup

- Method: 1:1 moderated interviews
- Duration: 45-60 minutes
- Recording: yes (with consent)
- Artifacts captured: verbatims, observed behaviors, confidence score

## Screening criteria

### Admin
- Has run at least 1 award or grant-style program in the last 18 months.
- Directly used software to configure forms, judges, and deadlines.

### Judge
- Judged at least 10 entries in any awards/grants/review platform in the last 24 months.
- Uses either desktop or mobile for evaluation sessions.

### Entrant
- Submitted at least 1 substantial application (award, grant, fellowship, media submission) in last 24 months.
- Spent >30 minutes on at least one submission.

---

## Persona 1: Award Program Admin

### Warmup
- Tell me about your role and how often you run award programs.
- What parts of award operations are you personally accountable for?

### Core interview script
- Walk me through the last time you set up an award from scratch.
- What does publishing mean to you: one action, or a readiness checklist?
- How do you track judging progress right now?
- Tell me about a moment something went wrong mid-program. What failed first?
- Where do you leave the platform and use spreadsheets or email?
- When do you typically add judges and team members?
- How do you feel about post-publish edits: should they be blocked, partially allowed, or fully allowed with logs?

### Deep probes
- Which status signals make you trust the platform?
- Which signals are missing when you feel uncertain?
- If you had to teach a colleague your setup process, where would they get stuck first?

### Wrap-up
- If you could remove one pain point forever, what would it be?
- What would make you recommend this platform to another organizer?

## Persona 2: Judge

### Warmup
- How many entries do you usually judge in one program?
- Which device do you use most while judging?

### Core interview script
- Describe your ideal judging session from start to finish.
- How do you decide which entry to score first?
- Do you prefer scoring one criterion at a time or all criteria together? Why?
- What breaks your flow while reviewing entries?
- How do you know your score/comment is saved?
- Tell me about a time you were unsure how to score an entry.
- Should judges see other judges' scores before submitting? Why or why not?

### Deep probes
- Would side-by-side content and scoring help you move faster?
- What do you do with entries that appear in the wrong category?
- Do you switch devices mid-session? If yes, what should persist?

### Wrap-up
- Most annoying part of judging platforms you have used?
- One thing that would make you finish assigned entries earlier?

---

## Persona 3: Entrant

### Warmup
- Tell me about the last application or submission you completed.
- Did you work alone or with teammates?

### Core interview script
- How do you decide whether a program is worth entering?
- What information do you need before starting the form?
- Was there a point where you almost abandoned the form?
- How do you currently collaborate with colleagues on one submission?
- What confirmation do you need to feel safe after clicking submit?
- Do you expect notifications by email, in-app, or both?

### Deep probes
- Which fields typically create confusion?
- What makes a submission form feel trustworthy versus risky?
- What deadline behaviors do you or your team show in the final 24 hours?

### Wrap-up
- One change that would increase your confidence while submitting?
- One change that would save your team the most time?
